The Battle of Teutoburg Forest

"Quintili Vare, legiones redde!“ ('Quintilius Varus, give me back my legions!') -Augustus Caeser

Beginings:

For Years the Romans had been attempting to control the Germanic Tribes. The Roman plan was to control the region which is now known as Germany. The Roman commander, for this mission was named Quintilius Varus. Around 9 A.D. Germanic tribes started an uprising that Varus wanted to put down. He marched forward with the 17th, 18th and 19th Legions intending to stop the uprising...

The Battle:

The Roman commander Varus was not moving with only his troops but also families. The Roman intention was also to settle regions of Germania with new Roman families. Because of this, the Roman soldiers were spread out in a long column like formation. Some estimates have the Roman troops streched out between 15 and 20 kilometers. The Germanic tribes slowly moved in on the Roman forces and slaughtered them over the course of a few days. It is estimated that between 15,000 and 20,000 Roman soldiers died. Varus ended up taking his own life.

Impact:

As the story goes, the Romans never again had any legions named the 17th, 18th and 19th Legions. Augustus Caeser, the emperor at the time of the battle, was heard saying "Quintili Vare, legiones redde!“ ('Quintilius Varus, give me back my legions!') as he walked the halls of his palace. Because of the defeat, the Romans would never colonize areas into Germania, modern day Germany. This prevented Germany from ever truly becoming 'Roman' and thus Germany would have a different outlook on the world than countries which were colonized by the Romans such as France. If this battle had gone differently, Germany would have been brought into the Roman sphere and the history of the 20th century, with World War One and Two may have been different...

Quick Facts

Date of Battle: 9 C.E.

Roman Commander: Publius Quinctilius Varus

Germanic Commander: Arminius

Winner of the battle: Germanic Tribes

Size of Germanic forces: 12,000 - 32,000

Roman Losses:15,000 - 20,000

Germanic Losses: ~1,000
